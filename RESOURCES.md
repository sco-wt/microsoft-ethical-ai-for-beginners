# Responsible AI Resources

## Knowledge

- [Microsoft AI for Beginners: Ethical and Responsible AI](https://github.com/microsoft/AI-For-Beginners/blob/main/lessons/7-Ethics/README.md)
  Starting point for this pathway. Use for: initial vocabulary, the six principles, and the Microsoft tools cited in the lesson.
- [Microsoft Responsible AI](https://www.microsoft.com/ai/responsible-ai)
  Microsoft's official page on responsible AI values, tools, and methods. Use for: checking the current wording of Microsoft's principles.
- [Microsoft Responsible AI Toolbox](https://github.com/microsoft/responsible-ai-toolbox)
  Open source toolkit for exploring data, understanding models, and analyzing errors. Use for: moving from principles to technical diagnostics.
- [NIST AI Risk Management Framework](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework)
  Official voluntary framework for managing AI risks in design, development, use, and evaluation. Use for: structuring audits around governance, mapping, measurement, and management.
- [European Commission: AI Act](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai)
  European reference on the risk-based approach and related obligations. Use for: understanding the European application context, without replacing legal advice.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [Microsoft Learn Community](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/)
  Microsoft community space. Use for: asking practical questions about Microsoft tools and Learn pathways.
- [NIST Trustworthy and Responsible AI Resource Center](https://airc.nist.gov/)
  Resources and use cases around the NIST AI RMF. Use for: comparing your practices with more operational examples.
