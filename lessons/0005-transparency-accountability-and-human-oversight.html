Lesson 0005

Transparency and Accountability

Course Home

Goal: distinguish "the user was told AI exists" from real transparency, oversight, appeal, and ownership.

The Core Idea

Transparency answers "what is happening and why?" Accountability answers "who can do something about it?"

Four Operational Questions

Disclosure

Do people know AI is involved before it affects them?

Explanation

Can the system provide a useful reason, not just a score?

Appeal

Can a person challenge a decision or ask for human review?

Owner

Is there a named team or person responsible for outcomes?

Logs

Can important decisions be reconstructed later?

Shutdown

Can the system be paused if monitoring shows harm?

Quick Check

1. "Tell users when AI generated a recommendation" mainly supports what?

2. "Name the team that can suspend the system" mainly supports what?

3. "Let a user request human review" is best described as what?

Practice Prompt

For a system you use, write one sentence naming the owner, the appeal path, and the condition that would trigger a pause.

Primary Reading

Read Microsoft's responsible AI principle descriptions and compare them with the glossary. Focus on the difference between an explanation and an accountable process.
