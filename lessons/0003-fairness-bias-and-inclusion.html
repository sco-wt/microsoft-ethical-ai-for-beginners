Lesson 0003

Fairness, Bias, and Inclusion

Course Home

Goal: learn to ask whether an AI system works differently for different groups, and whether the people most affected were considered.

The Core Idea

Bias is not only an ugly dataset. It can appear in labels, sampling, objectives, deployment context, feedback loops, and who gets ignored during design.

Three Fairness Questions

Representation

Are relevant groups present in the data and tested separately?

Outcome

Do error rates, false positives, or false negatives differ meaningfully by group?

Participation

Were affected people included when defining success and harm?

Quick Check

1. "False negatives are higher for older applicants" is primarily a problem of what?

2. "The training set barely includes rural users" points first to what?

3. "No disabled users were consulted before launch" points first to what?

Practice Prompt

For one use case, list three groups that may experience different outcomes. Then write the metric you would compare across those groups.

Primary Reading

Read about Fairlearn and the Microsoft lesson's fairness section. Focus on the habit of measuring group-level outcomes instead of assuming fairness from intent.
