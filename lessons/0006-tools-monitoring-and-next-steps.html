Lesson 0006

Tools, Monitoring, and Next Steps

Course Home

Goal: connect responsible AI principles to repeatable work: diagnostics before launch, monitoring after launch, and a record of decisions.

The Core Idea

Responsible AI is a lifecycle. A launch checklist is useful, but real responsibility continues through monitoring, incident response, review, and improvement.

Tooling Mindset

Fairness tools

Compare outcomes and error rates across groups.

Interpretability tools

Investigate which features or patterns drive predictions.

Error analysis

Find where the model fails, not just its average score.

Monitoring

Track drift, performance, complaints, incidents, and escalations.

Governance

Record owners, approvals, assumptions, and review dates.

Response

Know how to pause, fix, notify, and learn from failures.

Quick Check

1. Testing group-level error rates belongs mainly when?

2. Tracking drift and incidents belongs mainly when?

3. A review calendar without a named person is missing what?

Capstone Prompt

Choose one AI system and complete the Responsible AI Audit Checklist. The point is not to be perfect; the point is to make risks and responsibilities visible.

Primary Reading

Explore the Microsoft Responsible AI Toolbox. Focus on how tools can support, but not replace, human accountability.
