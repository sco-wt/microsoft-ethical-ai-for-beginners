Lesson 0002

Map AI Risks Before They Hide

Course Home

Goal: move from "this AI system could be risky" to a concrete map of who can be harmed, how, and where controls are needed.

The Core Idea

Responsible AI starts with a clear use case. If you cannot name the decision, the affected people, and the plausible harm, you cannot choose meaningful safeguards.

The Risk Map

1. Decision What does AI influence: ranking, prediction, generation, access, price, advice?
2. People Who is affected directly, indirectly, or invisibly?
3. Harm What can go wrong: denial, delay, exposure, manipulation, exclusion?
4. Control What evidence shows the risk is reduced, not merely named?

Quick Check

1. "The AI ranks loan applications" identifies which part of the risk map?

2. "A qualified person may be denied service" identifies what?

3. "Human review is required for low-confidence cases" is what?

Practice Prompt

Pick one AI use case and write one sentence in this format: "This AI influences X for Y people; the plausible harm is Z; the first control is W."

Primary Reading

Read the NIST AI Risk Management Framework overview. Focus on the move from abstract risk to govern, map, measure, and manage.
