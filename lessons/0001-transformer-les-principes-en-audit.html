Lesson 0001

Turn Principles into an Audit

Course Home

Goal: do more than list responsible AI principles. Turn them into useful questions you can ask about a real system.

The Core Idea

The Microsoft README presents AI as a powerful tool that can be misused. The practical response is not just to say "be ethical", but to check risks, evidence, and responsibilities.

The Six Angles

Fairness

Check whether some groups receive worse outcomes.

Reliability

Measure errors and their consequences in important situations.

Privacy

Limit data exposure and prevent misuse.

Inclusion

Include the people the system may forget or serve poorly.

Transparency

Make AI use visible, understandable, and contestable.

Accountability

Name the humans who can decide, correct, and take responsibility.

The 4-Step Method

1. Use Case Name the decision influenced by AI.
2. People List the affected people, especially vulnerable cases.
3. Risk Describe the plausible harm if the system fails.
4. Safeguard Ask for evidence: test, threshold, human review, appeal.

Quick Exercise

Scenario: a company uses AI to screen resumes before a human recruiter reads the best profiles.

1. Which principle pushes you to check whether women, older candidates, or certain backgrounds are ranked lower?

2. Which principle asks who can correct or suspend the automated screening?

3. Which principle asks you to tell candidates that AI is involved in the process?
