Responsible AI Principles

Lesson 0001

Responsible AI is not just a high-performing model. It is a system whose effects on people are understood, limited, documented, and monitored.

1

Fairness

Who could be disadvantaged by the data, the model, or the decision?

2

Reliability and Safety

What happens when the system is wrong, drifts, or encounters a rare case?

3

Privacy and Security

Which sensitive data enters, remains, leaves, or could be exposed?

4

Inclusion

Who might be excluded by language, accessibility, context, or data?

5

Transparency

Do people know that AI is involved, why it is used, and what its limits are?

6

Accountability

Who can explain, correct, suspend, or take responsibility for a system decision?

Quick Audit Grid

Step Question Expected Evidence
Use case Which decision or recommendation does AI influence? Short description of the workflow and human decisions.
People Who benefits, who loses, who may be poorly represented? List of affected groups and edge cases.
Risks What plausible harms exist if the system fails? Risk map: bias, error, leak, exclusion, opacity.
Safeguards Which controls actually reduce these risks? Tests, thresholds, human oversight, logging, appeal path.
Monitoring How will we know the system remains acceptable? Metrics, alerts, named owner, periodic review.
