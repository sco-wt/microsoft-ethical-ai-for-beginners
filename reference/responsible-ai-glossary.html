Responsible AI Glossary

Course Home

A compact vocabulary for the course. Use these terms consistently when auditing an AI system.

Core Terms

Term Meaning in this course
Responsible AI A way of designing, deploying, and monitoring AI so its effects on people are understood, limited, documented, and governed.
Fairness Checking whether groups experience materially different outcomes or error rates.
Bias A systematic pattern that can produce unfair or harmful outcomes.
Reliability The system behaves acceptably across normal, rare, and stressful conditions.
Privacy Sensitive data is minimized, protected, and not exposed through inputs, storage, logs, or outputs.
Inclusion Affected people can access, understand, and challenge the system, including people often ignored by default designs.
Transparency People can see when AI is involved, why it is used, and what its limits are.
Accountability Named humans or teams can explain, correct, pause, and take responsibility for the system.
Safeguard A control that reduces a risk and has evidence behind it.
Monitoring Ongoing checks after deployment for drift, errors, complaints, incidents, and changing context.
